# Question 9: Momentum Comparison

> **Problem:** Which has greater momentum: a 2-gram fly flying at 10 m/s or a 60-gram tennis ball moving at 1 m/s?

---

## Background

**Linear momentum** is the product of an object's mass and its velocity. It measures how much "motion" an object carries — a heavy slow object can have the same momentum as a light fast one.

$$\vec{p} = m\vec{v}$$

The magnitude is simply:

$$p = mv$$

Units: $\text{kg} \cdot \text{m/s}$

Momentum is a vector quantity, but since both objects here move in a single direction, we compare magnitudes only.

---

## Given Information

| Object | Mass | Speed |
|--------|------|-------|
| Fly | $m_f = 2\ \text{g} = 0.002\ \text{kg}$ | $v_f = 10\ \text{m/s}$ |
| Tennis ball | $m_b = 60\ \text{g} = 0.060\ \text{kg}$ | $v_b = 1\ \text{m/s}$ |

Note the unit conversion: grams must be converted to kilograms before substituting into the formula.

---

## Step 1 — Momentum of the Fly

$$p_f = m_f \times v_f = 0.002\ \text{kg} \times 10\ \text{m/s}$$

$$\boxed{p_f = 0.02\ \text{kg} \cdot \text{m/s}}$$

---

## Step 2 — Momentum of the Tennis Ball

$$p_b = m_b \times v_b = 0.060\ \text{kg} \times 1\ \text{m/s}$$

$$\boxed{p_b = 0.06\ \text{kg} \cdot \text{m/s}}$$

---

## Step 3 — Compare

$$\frac{p_b}{p_f} = \frac{0.06}{0.02} = 3$$

$$p_b = 3 \times p_f$$

**The tennis ball has 3 times the momentum of the fly.**

---

## Summary of Results

| Object | Mass | Speed | Momentum |
|--------|------|-------|----------|
| Fly | $0.002\ \text{kg}$ | $10\ \text{m/s}$ | $0.020\ \text{kg·m/s}$ |
| Tennis ball | $0.060\ \text{kg}$ | $1\ \text{m/s}$ | $0.060\ \text{kg·m/s}$ |

**Winner: the tennis ball**, with a momentum of $0.06\ \text{kg·m/s}$, which is 3 times greater than the fly's $0.02\ \text{kg·m/s}$.

---

## Interpretation

This result captures an important physical insight: **mass and velocity contribute equally to momentum**. The fly moves 10 times faster than the tennis ball, but the tennis ball is 30 times heavier. Since $30 > 10$, the mass advantage of the tennis ball outweighs the speed advantage of the fly by a factor of 3.

This is why a slow-moving heavy object can be far harder to stop than a fast-moving light one — stopping an object requires removing all of its momentum, regardless of how that momentum is distributed between mass and speed.

---

*Solved using the definition of linear momentum $p = mv$ with standard SI unit conversion.*
