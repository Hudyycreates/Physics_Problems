# 9. Refraction (Snell's Law)

### Problem Statement

A light ray travels from air ($n_1 = 1.00$) into glass ($n_2 = 1.50$). If the angle of incidence is $30^\circ$, what is the angle of refraction?

---

### Solution

#### 1. The Formula (Snell's Law)
$$n_1 \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \sin(\theta_2)$$

Where:
* $n_1 = 1.00$ (Air)
* $\theta_1 = 30^\circ$
* $n_2 = 1.50$ (Glass)
* $\theta_2 = ?$ (Angle of refraction)



---

#### 2. Step-by-Step Calculation

**Step A: Substitute values**
$$1.00 \cdot \sin(30^\circ) = 1.50 \cdot \sin(\theta_2)$$

**Step B: Simplify**
Since $\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$:
$$0.5 = 1.50 \cdot \sin(\theta_2)$$

**Step C: Isolate $\sin(\theta_2)$**
$$\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{0.5}{1.5} = \frac{1}{3}$$
$$\sin(\theta_2) \approx 0.3333$$

**Step D: Solve for $\theta_2$**
$$\theta_2 = \arcsin(0.3333)$$
$$\theta_2 \approx 19.47^\circ$$

---

### Final Result

The angle of refraction is:
**$$\theta_2 \approx 19.5^\circ$$**
