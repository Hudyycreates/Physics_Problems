# 1. Gauss's Law

### Problem Statement

A point charge of $+2\text{ C}$ is located at the origin. 

Calculate the electric flux ($\Phi_E$) through a spherical surface of radius $1\text{ m}$ centered at the origin.

---

### Solution

#### 1. Identify the Given Information

* **Enclosed Charge ($Q_{enc}$):** $+2\text{ C}$
* **Radius of Sphere ($r$):** $1\text{ m}$
* **Vacuum Permittivity ($\epsilon_0$):** $\approx 8.854 \times 10^{-12}\text{ C}^2/(\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2)$

---

#### 2. Apply Gauss's Law

Gauss's Law states that the net electric flux through any closed surface is equal to the total enclosed charge divided by the permittivity of free space:

$$\Phi_E = \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{Q_{enclosed}}{\epsilon_0}$$



---

#### 3. Analyze the Variables

One of the most important aspects of Gauss's Law is that the flux depends **only** on the charge enclosed. 

Even though the radius of the sphere is given ($1\text{ m}$), the flux will be the same for a sphere of any radius, as long as it still contains the $+2\text{ C}$ charge.

---

#### 4. Final Calculation

**Step A: Substitute the values**
$$\Phi_E = \frac{2\text{ C}}{8.854 \times 10^{-12}\text{ C}^2/(\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2)}$$

**Step B: Solve**
$$\Phi_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11}\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}$$

---

### Final Result

The electric flux through the spherical surface is:

**$$\Phi_E \approx 2.26 \times 10^{11}\text{ V}\cdot\text{m}$$**

*(Note: $\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}$ is equivalent to Volts-meters)*
