# 2. Ampere's Law

### Problem Statement

Two long, parallel wires are $10\text{ cm}$ apart and carry currents of $5\text{ A}$ in opposite directions. 

Calculate the magnitude and direction of the magnetic field ($B$) at a point midway between the wires.

---

### Solution

#### 1. Identify the Given Information

* **Current ($I_1$ and $I_2$):** $5\text{ A}$ each (opposite directions).
* **Total distance ($d$):** $0.1\text{ m}$ ($10\text{ cm}$).
* **Distance to midpoint ($r$):** $0.05\text{ m}$ ($5\text{ cm}$).
* **Permeability of free space ($\mu_0$):** $4\pi \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$.

---

#### 2. Determine Field Direction (Right-Hand Rule)

Let's assume Wire 1 is on the left (current going **up**) and Wire 2 is on the right (current going **down**).

1.  **Wire 1 (Up):** Using the right-hand rule, the magnetic field at the midpoint points **into the page**.
2.  **Wire 2 (Down):** Using the right-hand rule, the magnetic field at the midpoint also points **into the page**.

Since both fields point in the same direction at the midpoint, the total magnetic field is the **sum** of the two.



---

#### 3. Calculate Individual Field Magnitudes

The formula for the magnetic field of a long straight wire is:
$$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$$

**For Wire 1:**
$$B_1 = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \cdot 5}{2\pi \cdot 0.05} = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \cdot 5}{0.05} = 2 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$$

**For Wire 2:**
Since the current and distance are the same, $B_2 = B_1 = 2 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$.

---

#### 4. Final Calculation

$$B_{total} = B_1 + B_2$$
$$B_{total} = 2 \times 10^{-5} + 2 \times 10^{-5}$$
$$B_{total} = 4 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$$

---

### Final Result

The magnetic field at the midpoint is:

**$$B = 4 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$$**

* **Direction:** Perpendicular to the plane of the wires (directed "into the page" if using the example orientation).
