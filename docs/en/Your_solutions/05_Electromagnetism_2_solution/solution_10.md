# 10. Speed of Light in Media

### Problem Statement

What is the speed of light in a diamond, which has an index of refraction $n = 2.42$?

---

### Solution

#### 1. The Formula
The index of refraction ($n$) of a material is the ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum ($c$) to the speed of light in that material ($v$):

$$n = \frac{c}{v}$$

To find the speed in the material, we rearrange the formula:
$$v = \frac{c}{n}$$

---

#### 2. Final Calculation

**Step A: Identify constants**
* $c \approx 3.0 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$
* $n = 2.42$

**Step B: Solve**
$$v = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8}{2.42}$$
$$v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$$

---

### Final Result

The speed of light in a diamond is:
**$$v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8\text{ m/s}$$**

*(This is approximately $41\%$ of the speed of light in a vacuum.)*
