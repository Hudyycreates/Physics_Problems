# 4. Magnetic Torque

### Problem Statement

A rectangular loop of wire with dimensions $10\text{ cm}$ by $5\text{ cm}$ carries a current of $2\text{ A}$. A uniform magnetic field of $B = 0.3\text{ T}$ is applied parallel to the plane of the loop. 

What is the magnitude of the magnetic torque on the loop?

---

### Solution

#### 1. Identify the Given Information

* **Dimensions:** $0.1\text{ m}$ and $0.05\text{ m}$
* **Current ($I$):** $2\text{ A}$
* **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.3\text{ T}$
* **Number of turns ($N$):** $1$ (assumed)
* **Orientation:** Field is **parallel** to the plane of the loop.

---

#### 2. Calculate the Area ($A$)

$$A = \text{length} \times \text{width}$$
$$A = 0.1\text{ m} \times 0.05\text{ m} = 0.005\text{ m}^2$$

---

#### 3. Analyze the Angle ($\phi$)

The torque formula uses the angle $\phi$ between the **magnetic field** and the **normal vector** (perpendicular) of the loop's plane.

* If the field is **parallel** to the plane, it is **perpendicular** to the normal vector.
* Therefore, $\phi = 90^\circ$.
* $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$ (This results in the maximum possible torque).



---

#### 4. The Torque Formula

$$\tau = N \cdot I \cdot A \cdot B \cdot \sin(\phi)$$

**Final Calculation:**
$$\tau = 1 \cdot (2\text{ A}) \cdot (0.005\text{ m}^2) \cdot (0.3\text{ T}) \cdot 1$$

$$\tau = 2 \cdot 0.005 \cdot 0.3$$
$$\tau = 0.01 \cdot 0.3$$
$$\tau = 0.003\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}$$

---

### Final Result

The magnitude of the magnetic torque is:

**$$\tau = 3.0 \times 10^{-3}\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}$$**
