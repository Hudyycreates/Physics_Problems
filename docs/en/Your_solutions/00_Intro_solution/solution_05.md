# 5. Trigonometry

**Problem:** A vector $\vec{A}$ has a magnitude of $15$ and makes an angle of $\theta = 60^{\circ}$ with the horizontal axis. Calculate its horizontal ($A_x$) and vertical ($A_y$) components.

---

### **Understanding the Components**


To solve this, we resolve the vector into a right-angled triangle where:
* The **Hypotenuse** is the vector magnitude ($15$).
* The **Horizontal side ($A_x$)** is the adjacent side to the angle.
* The **Vertical side ($A_y$)** is the opposite side to the angle.

---

### **Mathematical Solution**

**Step 1: Calculate the horizontal component ($A_x$)**
$$A_x = 15 \cdot \cos(60^{\circ})$$

$$A_x = 15 \cdot 0.5$$

**$$A_x = 7.5$$**

---

**Step 2: Calculate the vertical component ($A_y$)**
$$A_y = 15 \cdot \sin(60^{\circ})$$

$$A_y = 15 \cdot 0.866$$

**$$A_y \approx 12.99$$**

---

### **Extra Notes**



* **Why $A_y > A_x$:** Since the angle ($60^{\circ}$) is greater than $45^{\circ}$, the vector is steeper, meaning more of its magnitude is directed vertically than horizontally.
* **Verification:** Use the Pythagorean theorem to check: $\sqrt{A_x^2 + A_y^2} \approx \sqrt{7.5^2 + 12.99^2} \approx \sqrt{56.25 + 168.74} = 15$.
* **Component Rule:** Always remember **SOH CAH TOA**:
  * **CAH**: Cosine = Adjacent ($A_x$) / Hypotenuse.
  * **SOH**: Sine = Opposite ($A_y$) / Hypotenuse.
