# 2. Systems of Equations

**Problem:** Find the values of $x$ and $y$ that satisfy both equations:
1. $2x + 3y = 12$
2. $x - y = 1$

---

### **Solution**

**By using (substitution method)** 

**Step 1: Isolate $x$ in Equation 2**
$$x - y = 1$$
$$x = y + 1$$
> **Note:** Isolate the variable with the simplest coefficient (1) to avoid fractions early on.


**Step 2: Substitute into Equation 1**
$$2(y + 1) + 3y = 12$$



**Step 3: Solve for $y$**

$$2y + 2 + 3y = 12$$
$$5y + 2 = 12$$
$$5y = 10$$
**$y = 2$**


**Step 4: Solve for $x$**
$$x = 2 + 1$$
**$x = 3$**
> **Note:** Plug the solved $y$ value back into the isolated $x$ equation from Step 1.


**Step 5: Proving**
* Equation 1: $2(3) + 3(2) = 6 + 6 = 12 \quad \checkmark$
* Equation 2: $3 - 2 = 1 \quad \checkmark$
