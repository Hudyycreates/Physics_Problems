# 4. Rearranging Formulas: The Pendulum

**Problem:** Rearrange the formula for the period of a simple pendulum, $T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$, to solve for $g$.

**Objective:** Isolate gravity ($g$) so we can calculate it using the pendulum's length ($L$) and period ($T$).

---

### **1. Step-by-Step Derivation**

**Step 1: Isolate the square root term**
Divide both sides by $2\pi$:

$$\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

---

**Step 2: Remove the square root**
Square both sides of the equation:

$$\left( \frac{T}{2\pi} \right)^2 = \frac{L}{g}$$



---

**Step 3: Expand the squared term**
Square the numerator and the denominator:

$$\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}$$

---

**Step 4: Isolate $g$**
Multiply both sides by $g$ and then multiply by the reciprocal of the left side:

$$g \cdot \left( \frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} \right) = L$$

$$g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$$

---

### **2. Variable Reference**

* **$g$**: Acceleration due to gravity ($m/s^2$)
* **$L$**: Length of the pendulum ($m$)
* **$T$**: Period of the pendulum ($s$)

---

### **3. Verification**

* **Dimensional Check:** The formula yields $\frac{m}{s^2}$, which correctly matches the units for acceleration. 
* **Logic:** As the period ($T$) increases, the calculated gravity ($g$) decreases, which aligns with physical observation.
