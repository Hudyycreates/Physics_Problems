# 4. Rearranging Formulas

**Problem:** Rearrange the formula for the period of a simple pendulum, $T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$, to solve for $g$.

**What we are finding:** We are deriving a formula to calculate the acceleration due to gravity ($g$) based on the known length of a pendulum ($L$) and its period ($T$).

---

### **Mathematical Solution**

**Step 1: Isolate the square root term**
$$\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

---

**Step 2: Remove the square root**
Square both sides of the equation:
$$\left( \frac{T}{2\pi} \right)^2 = \frac{L}{g}$$

---

**Step 3: Simplify the left side**
$$\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}$$

---

**Step 4: Isolate $g$**
Multiply both sides by $g$ and then multiply by the reciprocal $(\frac{4\pi^2}{T^2})$:

**$$g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$$**

---

### **Extra Notes**

* **Variables:** * $g$: Acceleration due to gravity ($m/s^2$).
  * $L$: Length of the pendulum ($m$).
  * $T$: Period of the pendulum ($s$).
* **Verification:** The units of the final formula $\frac{m}{s^2}$ correctly correspond to acceleration.
