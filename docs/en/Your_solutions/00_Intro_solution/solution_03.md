# 3. Proportionality

**Problem:** Using the Universal Law of Gravitation $F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$, determine the factor by which the force $F$ changes if the distance $r$ is doubled and both masses ($m_1$ and $m_2$) are halved.

---

### **Mathematical Solution**

**Step 1: Define the initial force**
$$F_{initial} = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

---

**Step 2: Apply the changes to the variables**
* New distance ($r_{new}$): $2r$
* New mass 1 ($m_{1new}$): $\frac{1}{2}m_1$
* New mass 2 ($m_{2new}$): $\frac{1}{2}m_2$

---

**Step 3: Calculate the new force ($F_{new}$)**
$$F_{new} = G \frac{(\frac{1}{2}m_1)(\frac{1}{2}m_2)}{(2r)^2}$$

---

**Step 4: Simplify the numerator and denominator**
Numerator:
$$(\frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2}) \cdot m_1 m_2 = \frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2$$

Denominator:
$$(2r)^2 = 4r^2$$

---

**Step 5: Solve for the change factor**
$$F_{new} = \frac{\frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2}{4 r^2}$$

$$F_{new} = (\frac{1}{4} \div 4) \cdot (G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2})$$

$$F_{new} = \frac{1}{16} F_{initial}$$



---

### **Extra Notes**

* **Factor of Change:** The resulting gravitational force decreases by a factor of **16**.
* **Inverse Square Law:** Doubling the distance $r$ alone decreases force by a factor of $2^2 = 4$.
* **Mass Relationship:** Gravitational force is directly proportional to the product of the masses. Reducing both masses by half multiplies the force by $\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4}$.
* **Cumulative Effect:** Combining these, the total change is $\frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{16}$.
