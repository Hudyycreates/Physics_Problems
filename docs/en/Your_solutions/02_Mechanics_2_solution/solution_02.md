# Problem 2 — Harmonic Motion

## Reading the Equation

Given: $x(t) = 0.2\cos(10\pi t)$

Standard SHM form: $x(t) = A\cos(\omega t)$

| Symbol | Meaning | Value |
|--------|---------|-------|
| $A$ | Amplitude | $0.2\ \text{m}$ |
| $\omega$ | Angular frequency | $10\pi\ \text{rad/s}$ |
| $m$ | Mass | $10\ \text{kg}$ |

---

## Part A — Spring Constant k

$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \implies k = m\omega^2$$

$$k = 10 \times (10\pi)^2 = 1000\pi^2$$

$$\boxed{k \approx 9{,}870\ \text{N/m}}$$

> The spring is very stiff — nearly 10,000 N per meter of stretch!

---

## Part B — Total Mechanical Energy E

$$E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2$$

$$E = \frac{1}{2} \times 1000\pi^2 \times (0.2)^2 = 20\pi^2$$

$$\boxed{E \approx 197.4\ \text{J}}$$

> Total energy stays constant — exchanged between potential and kinetic.

---

## Energy Recap

| Position | Potential Energy | Kinetic Energy |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| $x = A$ (max stretch) | $197.4\ \text{J}$ | $0\ \text{J}$ |
| $x = 0$ (center) | $0\ \text{J}$ | $197.4\ \text{J}$ |
| Anywhere else | Partial | Partial |
