# Problem 12 — Work and Energy with a Constant Force

## Given

$$m = 2\ \text{kg}, \quad \vec{F} = (6,\ 2)\ \text{N},
\quad \vec{v}_0 = (1,\ -1)\ \text{m/s}, \quad \vec{r}_0 = (0,0)\ \text{m}$$

---

## Part 1 — Acceleration

$$\boxed{\vec{a} = \frac{\vec{F}}{m} = (3,\ 1)\ \text{m/s}^2}$$

---

## Part 2 — Velocity

$$\boxed{\vec{v}(t) = (1 + 3t,\ -1 + t)\ \text{m/s}}$$

---

## Part 3 — Position

$$\boxed{\vec{r}(t) = \left(t + \frac{3}{2}t^2,\ -t + \frac{1}{2}t^2\right)\ \text{m}}$$

---

## Part 4 — Trajectory

Parametric curve: $(x, y) = (t + \frac{3}{2}t^2,\ -t + \frac{1}{2}t^2)$ for $t \geq 0$.

The path curves toward the force direction $(3, 1)$ m/s².

---

## Part 5 — Work at $t = 3$ s

$$\Delta\vec{r} = (16.5,\ 1.5)\ \text{m}$$

$$W = \vec{F} \cdot \Delta\vec{r} = 6(16.5) + 2(1.5)$$

$$\boxed{W = 102\ \text{J}}$$

---

## Part 6 — Work–Energy Theorem

$$KE(0) = \tfrac{1}{2}(2)(1^2+1^2) = 2\ \text{J}$$

$$KE(3) = \tfrac{1}{2}(2)(10^2+2^2) = 104\ \text{J}$$

$$\Delta KE = 102\ \text{J} = W \quad \checkmark$$

> Work done equals the change in kinetic energy — exactly as the theorem predicts.
