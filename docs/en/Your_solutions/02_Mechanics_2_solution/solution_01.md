# Problem 1 — Gravitational Dependence

## The Formula

The period of a simple pendulum is:

$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| $T$ | Period (seconds) |
| $L$ | Length of pendulum (m) |
| $g$ | Gravitational acceleration (m/s²) |

---

## Part A — Period on the Moon

**Given:**
- $T_{Earth} = 4\ s$
- $g_{Moon} = \dfrac{g_{Earth}}{6}$

**Key Insight:** The length $L$ stays the same, so we take the ratio:

$$\frac{T_{Moon}}{T_{Earth}} = \sqrt{\frac{g_{Earth}}{g_{Moon}}} = \sqrt{6}$$

$$T_{Moon} = 4 \times \sqrt{6}$$

$$\boxed{T_{Moon} \approx 9.8\ \text{seconds}}$$

> The pendulum swings ~2.45× slower on the Moon.

---

## Part B — Required Length for T = 1s on Earth

**Given:**
- $T = 1\ s$
- $g = 9.8\ m/s^2$

$$1 = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{9.8}}$$

$$\frac{L}{9.8} = \frac{1}{4\pi^2}$$

$$L = \frac{9.8}{4\pi^2}$$

$$\boxed{L \approx 0.248\ \text{m} \approx 24.8\ \text{cm}}$$

> A pendulum less than 25 cm long ticks once per second on Earth!
