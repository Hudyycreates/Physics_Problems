# 5. Field Levitation

### Problem Statement

What electric field strength is required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity at the level of the Earth's surface? 

**Given Constants:**
* **Mass of proton ($m_p$):** $\approx 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* **Charge of proton ($e$):** $\approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Gravitational acceleration ($g$):** $\approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$

---

### Solution

#### 1. Analyze the Forces

For a proton to "levitate," it must be in a state of equilibrium. This means the net force acting on it must be zero. There are two primary forces at play:

1.  **Gravitational Force ($F_g$):** Acts vertically **downward**.
2.  **Electric Force ($F_e$):** Must act vertically **upward** to oppose gravity.



---

#### 2. Establish the Equilibrium Condition

To levitate, the magnitude of the upward electric force must equal the magnitude of the downward gravitational force:

$$F_e = F_g$$

Substituting the formulas for each force ($F_e = qE$ and $F_g = mg$):

$$e \cdot E = m_p \cdot g$$

---

#### 3. Rearrange to Solve for Electric Field ($E$)

To find the electric field strength, we isolate $E$:

$$E = \frac{m_p \cdot g}{e}$$

---

#### 4. Final Calculation

**Step A: Substitute the known values**
$$E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}) \cdot (9.8 \text{ m/s}^2)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}}$$

**Step B: Perform the multiplication in the numerator**
$$E = \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}$$

**Step C: Divide to find the field strength**
$$E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$$

---

### Final Result

The required electric field strength is:

**$$E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$$**

* **Direction:** The field must point **vertically upward**. Since a proton is positively charged, the force it experiences is in the same direction as the electric field.
