# 10. Lorentz Force acting on Wire

### Problem Statement

A straight wire $2.0\text{ m}$ long carries a current of $10\text{ A}$. It is placed in a uniform magnetic field of $B = 0.5\text{ T}$. 

Calculate the magnetic force on the wire if the angle ($\theta$) between the wire and the magnetic field is:
* **a)** $90^\circ$
* **b)** $45^\circ$
* **c)** $0^\circ$

---

### Solution

#### 1. Identify the Given Information

* **Length of the wire ($L$):** $2.0\text{ m}$
* **Current ($I$):** $10\text{ A}$
* **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.5\text{ T}$

---

#### 2. The Formula for Magnetic Force on a Wire

The magnetic force ($F$) acting on a current-carrying wire in a uniform magnetic field is given by the formula:

$$F = I L B \sin(\theta)$$

Where:
* $I$ is the current.
* $L$ is the length of the wire.
* $B$ is the magnetic field strength.
* $\theta$ is the angle between the direction of the current and the magnetic field.



---

#### 3. Step-by-Step Calculations

Before calculating each case, let's determine the constant part of the equation ($I \cdot L \cdot B$):
$$I \cdot L \cdot B = (10\text{ A}) \cdot (2.0\text{ m}) \cdot (0.5\text{ T}) = 10\text{ N}$$

Now, we apply this to the three different angles:

**Case a) $\theta = 90^\circ$ (Perpendicular)**
* $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$
* $F = 10 \cdot 1 = 10\text{ N}$
* *Observation: This is the maximum possible force.*

**Case b) $\theta = 45^\circ$**
* $\sin(45^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707$
* $F = 10 \cdot 0.707 = 7.07\text{ N}$

**Case c) $\theta = 0^\circ$ (Parallel)**
* $\sin(0^\circ) = 0$
* $F = 10 \cdot 0 = 0\text{ N}$
* *Observation: When the wire is parallel to the field, no magnetic force is generated.*

---

### Final Result Summary

| Angle ($\theta$) | Calculation | Force Magnitude ($F$) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **90°** | $10 \cdot \sin(90^\circ)$ | **$10\text{ N}$** |
| **45°** | $10 \cdot \sin(45^\circ)$ | **$7.07\text{ N}$** |
| **0°** | $10 \cdot \sin(0^\circ)$ | **$0\text{ N}$** |

> **Key Takeaway:** The magnetic force is highly dependent on the orientation of the wire. It is strongest when the wire is perpendicular to the field and disappears entirely when the wire is parallel to the field.
